The City That Changed Overnight

How the COVID-19 pandemic reshaped crime patterns in San Francisco, shifting activity from public hubs to private settings.

San Francisco is a city known for its bustling tourist spots and packed restaurants. But in March 2020, the city took a sharp turn. Streets emptied, people retreated to their homes, and the city became unrecognizable. As tourism plummeted, crime patterns shifted along with the population's daily routines.
